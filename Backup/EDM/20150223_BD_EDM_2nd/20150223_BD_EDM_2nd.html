SCG has researched and developed the innovative building materials for housing that add value for consumers and respond to consumers' demands in all dimensions. This provides comfortable living, and is certified SCG eco value label. About the eco-friendly products, SCG focuses on maximizing resource efficiency, starting from manufacturing process to transport, and product use such as SCG SmartWood that helps reduce power consumption in the house, SmartBOARD - the light weight partition system that are manufactured under a highly controlled environment and SCG Insulation that made of fiber glass wool which is inflammable, not harmful to users, help consumer to save energy and create a comfort of living.

Construction industry in Myanmar has been growing an exponential rate of 20% since year 2010. The use of construction materials in Myanmar in the past was mainly for functional until the third quarter of year 2013 when more high-rise hotels and buildings in Yangon have been built with more design. Initiative on green building project has also started in Myanmar.

To serve the rising demand of construction industry in Myanmar, SCG has invested US$400 million in a new cement plant in Mawlamyine, Myanmar since last year. The facility, scheduled to complete by mid-2016, will have a capacity of 1.8 million tpa and will feature a waste-to-energy system. And always forefront of the trend, SCG has started introducing line of eco products to Myanmar market!

“We had huge success from exhibiting in the Myanmar Build & Decor 2014. At SCG Booth, we also received excellent response from visitors who were mainly key decision makers and influencers. SCG products were better known to the market from this exhibition and we look forward to a greater success for the second Myanmar Build & Décor this year” ,said Mr.  Sompong Jaengjetsada, Sales and Marketing Manager of SCG Cement Materials Co., Ltd. -  Myanmar. At Myanmar Build & Décor 2015, SCG will introduce to the market its Environmentally-friendly commitment line of product.
